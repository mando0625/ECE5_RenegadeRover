What does PID stand for? What is the purpose of the P, the I, and the D? What steps did you take to choose the best values for your robot? Explain this like you would for someone taking this course next year.

  PID stands for proportional integral derivative. The purpose of P is to avoid steady-state error, and the purpose of I is to offer an accumulated offset that should have been corrected in the previous step, and the purpose of D is to correct for previous errors.
